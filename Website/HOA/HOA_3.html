Dravidian Architecture

Greetings of the day everyone, this is Khushi and I am pursuing Diploma in Architectural Assistantship. I hope you liked my previous blog. So, in this blog, We talk about Dravidian Architecture.

DRAVIDIAN ARCHITECTURE is emerged is the southern part of the Indian sub-continent or south India thousands of years ago. It is built by Dravidian people, such structures primarily consist pyramid shaped temples called kovils, kind of design. Mentioned as one of the three styles of temple building in the ancient book vastu shastra, the majority of the existing structures are located in the south Indian states of Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, Kerala and Andhra Pradesh, various kingdoms and empire such as THE CHOLAS, THE CHERA, THE PANDYAS, THE PALLAVAS, THE GANGAS, THE RASHTRAKUTAS, THE CHALUKYAS, THE HOYSALAS AND TE VIJAYANAGARA EMPIRE. Among other have made substantial contribution to the evolution of Dravidian architecture.

CHARACTERISTICS

• DRAVIDIAN ARCHITECTURE developed into its own form and tradition.
• Temples were not only the place of worship but also center of learning. Along with elaborate religious rituals, festivals, performances of dance etc.
• Dravidian being the craftsman, temple was designed most elegantly with great interest to display ornamentation and fineart that covered every part of the temple.
• Dravidian architecture which date back from 600 AD to 1000 AD may broadly be divided into rock cut monuments to structural monuments.

DRAVIDIAN ARCHITECTURE IS SUB DEVIDED INTO 5 STYLES.

1. CHOLA ARCHITECTURE
2. PANDYA ARCHITECTURE
3. HOYSALA ARCHITECTURE
4. NAYAK ARCHITECTURE
5. PALLAVA ARCHITECTURE

Chola Architecture(900–1050)AD

The cholas are the equally great builders vimana at Tanjore built by raja chola 1 and by his son Rajendra chola. A large abacus, simple brackets and plasters between niches and makara toranas with foliated tails fill the prospect at this stage of the dravida style.


BRIHADESHWAR

Location – Thanjavur, Tamil Nadu

Year – 1010 AD

Present age – 1018

Built by – RAJA raja Chola

Time taken – 5 years

Dimensions – 216 feet wide and 66 meters high

Part of UNESCO world heritage site as “great living chola temple”.

Pandya Architecture (1100-1350)AD

The pandya temples are about as great. Those at shrinagaram, Chidambaram, kumbakonam and tirumala manai possess gigantic towers and vimanas as large as chola temples. But the actual pandya shrines are dwarfed by the walls and gates.

MEENAKSHI TEMPLE

Location - Madurai, Tamil Nadu

Year – 600 AD

Built by – kulasekara pandya

Height – 9 stories

Time taken – 10 years

Hoysala Architecture (1350-1565)AD

Developed under the rule of Hoysala empire between the 11th and the 14th centuries. Hoysala influence was at its peak on the 13th century chennakesava temple at belur, Hoysaleswara temple at Halebidu etc.

VIRUPAKSHI TEMPLE

Location – Hampi, karnataka

Year – 740 AD

Present age – 1278

Built by – lokmahadevi, The queen of vikramaditya

Time taken – 12 years

Nayak Architecture (1600AD ONWARDS)

The nayakas of madura established their kingdom and became a leading builder in the land. The most important kind and builder among them was Thirumalai nayak. The well-known vasanta and padumandapam in front of the meenakshi temple has flat roofed corridors with three aisles.

Pallava Architecture

Pallava art and architecture represent the early stage of Dravidian art and architecture. The first stone and mortar temples of India were constructed during pallava rule and were based on earlier brick and timber prototypes. The shore temple and the pancha rathas of the mahabalipuram.
