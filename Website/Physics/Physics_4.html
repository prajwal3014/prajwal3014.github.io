Newton's laws of motion

Greetings of the day everyone, this is Ayush Rana. I hope you all liked my previous blog. So, in this blog, I am going to throw some light on the topic “Newton's laws of motion”.

Newton's laws of motion are of major importance in classical mechanics. A large number of principles and results may be derived from Newton's laws of motion. These laws relate to the type of motion of a system that results from a given set of forces.

First law of motion

"If the vector sum of all the the forces acting on a particle is zero then and only then the particle remains unaccelerated."

The above statement may also be written as "→a = 0 if and only if → F = 0"
(where a represents acceleration, F represents force and → represents vector).

Second law of motion

"The acceleration of a particle as measured from an initial frame is given by the vector sum of all the forces acting on the particle divided by its mass."

In symbols above given statement a can be written as "→a = →F/m"
(where a represents acceleration, F represents force , → represents vector and m represents mass).

Third law of motion

"If a body A exerts a force →F on another body B, then B exerts a force →F on A."

Thus, the forces exerted by A on B and that by B on A are equal in magnitude but opposite in direction.
