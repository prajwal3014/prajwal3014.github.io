Introduction to the world of physics

Greetings of the day everyone, this is Ayush Rana and, in this series of blogs, I am going to give you a full tutorial on the world of physics.


There are various natural phenomena such as the winds, the waters, the plannets, the sun, the function of human body, the energy coming from nuclear processes and many more.
There are a large number of events taking place in nature. And these could be explained by physics.
So physics is basically the study of nature and its laws. We expect all the above mentioned events take place according to some laws and revealing these laws of nature from the observed events is physics.


The great physicist Dr R. P. Feynman has given a great perspective on 'what is physics?'.
According to him physics can be explained with the help of a game of chess. Suppose we do not know the rules of chess but are allowed to watch the moves of the players. If we watch the game for a long time, we may make out some of the rules. With the knowledge of these rules we may try to understand why a player made this particular move.
However this may be a difficult task. Even if we know all the rules of chess, it is not easy to understand all the complications of the game in a given situation and predict a correct move. One may guess at a wrong rule by partially watching the game. The experienced may make a use of a rule for the first time and observer may get surprised.
Because of the new move some of the rules guessed at may prove to be wrong and the observer will frame new rules.


Physics goes the same way. The nature around us is like a big chess game played by nature.
The events in the nature are like the moves of a great game. We are allowed to watch the events and guess at basic rules according to the events take place. We may come accross some new events which may contradict the rules guessed earlier and we may have to declare the old rule inapplicable and discover new rules.
