Friction

Greetings of the day everyone, this is Ayush Rana. I hope you all liked my previous blog. So, in this blog, I am going to throw some light on the topic “Friction”.

When two bodies are kept in contact. Each body exrts a contract force on other due to the em forces between the charged particles at the surface of the bodies. The magnitude of contact forces acting on the two bodies are equal but their directions are opposite and hence the contact forces obey Newton's third law.

The direction of the contact force acting on a particular body is not necessarily pependicular to the contact surface. We can resolve this contact force into two components, one perpendicular to the contact surface and other parallel to it. The perpendicular component of the contact force is called the normal force and the parallel component is called friction.

Friction can operate between a given pair of solids, between a solid and a fluid or between a pair of fluids. When two solid bodies slip over each other, the force of friction is called kinectic friction. when two bodies do not slip on each other, the force of friction between them is called static friction.

Kinetic friction

When two bodies in contact move with respect to each other, rubbing the surfaces in contact, the friction in between them is called kinetic friction. The directions of the frictional forces are such that the relative slipping is opposed by the friction.

Static friction

Frictional forces can also act between two bodies which are in contact but are not sliding with respect to each other. The friction in such cases is called static friction. A great example can be when someone is pushing a heavy object placed on a ground surface and it is not moving from it's place due to its weight then in this case the static friction is exerted between the object and ground.
