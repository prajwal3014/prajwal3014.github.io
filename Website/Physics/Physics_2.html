Kinematics

Greetings of the day everyone, this is Ayush Rana. I hope you all liked my previous blog. So, in this blog, I am going to throw some light on the topic “Kinematics”.


Kinematics is the branch of mechnical physics which deals with the geometry of objects in motion around us.
Now a great question arises that what is motion?
Motion is basically a combined property of the object under study and the observer. There is no meaning of rest or motion without the viewer. Nothing is in absolute motion or rest everything is relative to the viewer or observer. A great example of above given statement is the sun is moving with respect to the observer on the earth and the earth is moving with respect to the sun.


We need a frame of reference to describe the motion of a particle or object by observing the change in position of particle with respect to the frame of reference. A very easy way to pick up aframe of reference is to choose three mutually perpendicular axes namely X-Y-Z axes. The coordinates of particle on these ases specifies the position of particle with respect to that frame. If all three coordinates x.y and z of the particle remain unchanged as the time passes, we say the paricle is at rest with respect to this frame. And if one or more coordinates changes with time, then we can say that particle is in motion with respect to this frame.

So, this is all about the Kinematics. Thank you to be so patient.
