The Forces

Greetings of the day everyone, this is Ayush Rana. I hope you all liked my previous blog. So, in this blog, I am going to throw some light on the topic “The Forces”.

Force can be discribed as an interaction between two objects.It is an action that tends to maintain or change the motion of a object. One of the various examples of force is when you push or pull some object you exert a certain force on it and it can be seen as an interation between yourself and the object.

Force is a vector quantity(i.e. having magnitude and direction) and if more than one forces act on a particle or object then we can find the resultant force using law of vector addition.The SI unit of force is newton. Approximately it is the force needed to hold a body of mass 102g near the earth's surface.

Various forces of the universe can be catogarized into four forces of nature namely gravitational, electromagnetic, nuclear and weak forces.

Gravitational force is the attraction of two bodies with each other by the virtue of their masses. This is the most important binding factor of heavenly bodies in the universe.

Electromagnetic force is the force exerted between two particles by the virtue of their charges(both positive or negative). The atoms combine to form molecules due to the electromagnetic forces. A lot of atomic and molecular phenomena result from elctromagnetic forces between the subatomic particles.

Nuclear forces are the forces that result in the stability of a nucleus of atom. These are attractive in nature and exerted to hold the nucleus stable due to the instability caused by electromagnectic forces. Radioactivity, Nuclear energy(fission, fussion) etc result from nuclear forces

Weak forces are the forces which are responsible for the changes such as a neutron can change itself into a proton or a proton can also change itself into a neutron.

So, this is all about the Forces. Thank you to be so patient.
