Crystal Lattice :

A regular 3D arrangement of points[constituent particles] in space is called a crystal Lattice.

Characteristics of a Crystal Lattice:-
• Each point in a crystal lattice represents one constituent particle which may be an atom, a molecule, or an ion.
• Each point in a lattice is called a lattice point.
• Lattice points are joined by straight lines to bring out the geometry of lattice.

Unit cell :

It is the smallest portion of a crystal lattice. When it is repeated in different directions generates the entire lattice.

1. a, b, c : Three edges(length)
2. a’, b’, c’ : Angles between edges


Types of Unit Cell :

• Primitive unit cell : Constituent particles are present only on the corner of the unit cell.

• Body Centred unit cell : When a unit cell contains one particle at its body center beside the ones that are at its corners.

• Face Centered unit cell : When a unit cell contains one particle at Centre of each face beside the ones that are at its corners.

• End Centered unit cell : In such a unit cell, one particle is present at the center of any 2 opposite faces beside the ones present at its corners.

Parameters & variations of 7 crystals

Bravais Lattice :

We have totally 14 types of unit cells available naturally under 7 Crystal systems.

These 14 unit cells are called bravais lattice.

Contribution of lattice points in unit cell

1. Corner : 1 corner atom is contributing equally in 8 unit cells. So in 1 unit cell, the contribution is (1/8).
2. Body Centre : If atom is present at body Centre its contribution in a unit cell is 1.
3. Face Centre : If atom is present at face center, then its contribution in a unit cell is (1/2).
4. Edge Centre : if atom is present at edge center then its contribution in a unit cell is (1/4).

Number of atoms in unit cell :

1. Primitive Cubic unit cell :
• In a simple cubic unit cell, each corner atom is shared between 8 unit cells. So contribution of a particle which is at corner = 1/8 for a unit cell.
• Total no of atoms in one unit cell
=> No. Of atoms at corner × contribution of particles at corner → 8 ×1/8 = 1

2. Body Centered cubic unit cell [BCC] :
• Body center wholly belongs to the unit cell in which it is present.
=> 8 corners × 1/8 per corner atoms = 1 atom
=> 1 body centre atom = 1 atom
Total no. Of atom per unit cell = 2 atoms

3. Face Centered Cubic unit cell [FCC] :
• Each atom locked at the face center is shared between 2 adjacent unit cells and only ½ of each atom belongs to unit cell.
=> 8 corners atoms × 1/8 per carbon atom = 1 atom
=> 6 face centered atoms × 1/2 atom per unit cell = 3
Total no. Of atoms per unit cell = 4 atoms
