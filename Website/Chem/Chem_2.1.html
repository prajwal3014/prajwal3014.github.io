Lecture 1 of Solid States


• As we know, matter can exist in 3 states: Solid, liquid and Gas. In this chapter we’ll study only about solid state.
• Every substance have 2 opposite forces in it :-
1. The attractive forces between the particles which tends to keep the particles closer.
2. The thermal energy which tends to keep them apart by making them move faster.


GENERAL CHARACTERISTICS OF SOLID STATES:-
1. They have definite mass, shape and volume.
2. Intermolecular forces are strong.
3. Intermolecular distances are short.
4. They are incompressible and rigid.
5. Their constituent particles {atoms, molecules or ions} have fixed volume and can only oscillate about their mean positions.

Types Of Solids


1. CRYSTALLINE SOLIDS
   It consists of a large number of small crystals each of them having a definite characteristic geometrical shape.
   It has long range regular pattern of arrangement of particles.
   For e.g:- NaCl crystals, Quartz

2. AMORPHOUS SOLIDS
   It consists of particles of irregular shape and has short range order.
   For e.g:- Quartz glass
